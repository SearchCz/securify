# **Observer Authority**

Focus determines **what** an observer watches.

Authority determines **whether the observer is currently permitted to respond.**

This distinction is one of the defining principles of Securify.

A condition may deserve awareness without immediately deserving interruption.

Authority allows homeowners to delay, suppress, or authorize responses according to their own priorities.

---

# **Why Authority Exists**

Many conditions become meaningful long before they deserve a notification.

A garage door opened to unload groceries should not immediately produce an alert.

A person walking through the living room should not generate repeated announcements.

A brief water leak detector activation may simply be a splash.

In each case, the condition is real.

The observer should activate.

But the homeowner may not yet wish to be interrupted.

Authority exists to distinguish **awareness** from **interruption**.

---

# **Persistence**

The most common form of Authority is **Persistence**.

Persistence requires that an observer’s Focus remain active for a specified period of time before any response is permitted.

If the observed condition resolves before that time expires, no response is generated.

For example:

- A garage door may be opened for five minutes while unloading a vehicle.
- A door may remain unlocked briefly while carrying groceries.
- Motion may occur momentarily as someone passes through a room.

These are real conditions.

The observer activates immediately.

But because they do not persist, the homeowner is never interrupted.

Persistence dramatically reduces unnecessary notifications while ensuring that genuinely lingering conditions still receive attention.

---

# **Gating**

Authority may also depend upon a homeowner-defined gating condition.

A gate is any Indigo device whose state determines whether responses are currently authorized.

Examples include:

- Someone is home.
- Everyone is in bed.
- It is dark outside.
- Vacation mode is active.
- Quiet hours are in effect.

If the gate is not satisfied, the observer may remain active indefinitely without producing a response.

As soon as the gate becomes satisfied, the observer immediately becomes eligible to respond, provided its other Authority requirements have also been met.

---

# **Combining Persistence and Gating**

Persistence and gating work together.

For example:

A garage door observer might require:

- the garage door to remain open for fifteen minutes, **and**
- the homeowner to be away from home.

The observer activates as soon as the garage door is opened.

It becomes authorized only after both conditions have been satisfied.

This allows Securify to model real-world expectations without creating unnecessary interruptions.

---

# **Authority Is Dynamic**

Authority is continuously evaluated.

An observer may become authorized long after it first activated.

Likewise, it may lose authorization if a gating condition changes.

Observers therefore adapt automatically as the home’s circumstances evolve.

No additional automation is required.

---

# **Observer Activation and Authority**

An observer becoming **On** does not mean it is authorized to respond.

It simply indicates that the condition it watches currently exists.

Authority is evaluated independently.

This distinction is intentional.

It allows homeowners to see that an observer is active while still preventing unnecessary notifications until the configured Authority requirements have been satisfied.

---

# **Authority Reduces Noise**

Many notification systems ask only one question:

“Did this happen?”

Securify asks a second question:

**“Has this condition earned the homeowner’s attention?”**

That simple distinction dramatically reduces notification fatigue.

Brief, expected conditions are quietly ignored.

Persistent, meaningful conditions eventually receive the attention they deserve.

The homeowner decides where that threshold lies.

Securify simply applies that policy consistently.

---

# **A Thoughtful Home**

Authority ensures that Securify responds thoughtfully rather than reactively.

Observers recognize changing conditions immediately.

Authority determines when those conditions deserve to interrupt the homeowner.

The result is a home that remains continuously aware while respecting the homeowner’s attention.
